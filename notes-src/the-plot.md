after sylvia wynter. in plantation economies, the provision ground, the small plot where enslaved people grew their own food outside the plantation's system, was a site of resistance, alternative economies, and self-definition. wynter reads the plot as the model for any practice that operates outside dominant systems of value.

[[tool-farming]] is plot logic. the personal cli, the self-hosted garden, or a zero-dependency zine tool, these are plots, not products. they produce sustenance and selfhood outside the platform economy's logic.

the plot does not scale. that is the point.
