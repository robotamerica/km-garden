chicana cultural theorist, poet, and philosopher from the us-mexico border. her borderlands/la frontera: the new mestiza (1987) names the psychic, cultural, and geographic space between worlds as its own territory, more biodiverse and generative than either side that created it.

her theory of [[nepantla]], the nahuatl word for the in-between space of transformation, is the philosophical ground beneath [[ecotonal-practice]]. nepantla is not confusion or failure; it is the site where old frameworks break down and new ones become possible.

she writes in code-switching spanish and english prose-poetry, itself a situated, ecotonal act. the most essential addition to my lineage. her absence was a gap, not an omission.
