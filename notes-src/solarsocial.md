[solarsocial]() is a framework and experiment for thinking about media and networked communication through **solar logic**: slow, ambient, renewable, and intermittent.

it's not just about powering servers with sunlight (which at this point i cannot afford). it's about designing systems with the cyclical rhythms of the planet in mind insiting on presence over performance, and intention over interaction.

a solarsocial approach is **seasonal**, **asynchronous**, and **gentle**.

## conceptual shifts

- infinite scroll → finite, seasonal publishing
- cloud-hosted → locally-hosted or intermittently connected
- engagement-optimized → reflection-optimized
- fomo → jomo (joy of missing out)
- extractive attention economy → regenerative attention ecology

## energy as interface

uptime becomes tidal, sunlight becomes a signal, and if the sun's not shining, the site can nap. **embrace intermittence**.

> build something that goes offline sometimes. build something that listens to the sun.

this connects directly to [[soft-stack-ecology]]: creating systems as living things, not always-on machines. this leads me to [[tool-farming]], which is about building simple iterative tools that help us connect without always needing to be present and online: the tool is the connection.
