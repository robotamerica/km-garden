economist and author of small is beautiful (1973). argued for intermediate and appropriate technology: human-scale systems, tools scaled to the human body and the local community, not to corporate ambition or abstract efficiency.

small is not a size but a relationship. schumacher's question: what is the appropriate scale for this tool, this context? not: how do i scale this to reach everyone? the second question produces platforms. the first produces tools i can actually use and maintain alone.

close intellectual neighbour to [[illich]]. both writing in 1973, both asking whether the tools of industrial society were actually serving the people who used them. [[sufficiency]] is the direct inheritance.
